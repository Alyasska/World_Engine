# The Siege of Vareth

*Year 12 of the Long Wars — forty days that determined who would rule the Northern Reach.*

---

## What Happened

The Compact's allied forces encircled Vareth in early Year 12 of the Long Wars. Aran Voss's father died on the third day of the siege. Aran, twenty-three, took command of a starving city — food stores set for 28,000, population sheltering 40,000.

On the fortieth day a late-season flood cut the Compact army's supply line. They retreated. No decisive battle was fought. Both sides described the outcome as the other's failure.

---

## Causes

The Compact had been attempting to neutralize the Northern Reach's control of the northern mountain passage for years. The siege was the culmination of a decade of border pressure and failed negotiations.

---

## Consequences

The siege's end became the founding myth of Aran Voss's rule. He did not win — the river won — but the story was written as strength. The Compact's retreat created a window for the eventual Treaty of Solmark negotiations.

---

## Notes

*The flood is treated in official Northern Reach history as a result of Voss's strategic patience. Mira Solhand's correspondence describes it as luck and says Voss knows it. This discrepancy is a live story thread.*

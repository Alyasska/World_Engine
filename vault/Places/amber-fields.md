# Amber Fields

*The fields were here before the wars and will be here after them.*

---

## Description

The village sits at the edge of a wide plain that catches the late afternoon light and turns golden from midsummer to first frost. That is where the name comes from — not the grain itself, which is ordinary, but the quality of light when it ripens.

Solan grew up here. He left at eighteen and has been back twice. Both times the village looked exactly the same, which he finds either comforting or troubling depending on the day.

---

## Notes

*Amber Fields is the origin point of the main heroic narrative. The contrast between this peaceful agricultural place and the wars Solan enters is deliberate.*

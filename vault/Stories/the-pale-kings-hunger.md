# The Pale King's Hunger

*The arrival of something that does not explain itself, told by those who could not explain it either.*

---

## Structure

Fragmentary documents: garrison reports, a merchant's journal, intercept letters, and an anonymous account whose author is never identified. The Pale King appears in third person throughout. He never speaks directly.

---

## Notes

*The horror here is not supernatural — it is the horror of encountering a purpose you cannot read. The Pale King wants something. What he wants is never stated. The story ends before he achieves it. That is intentional.*

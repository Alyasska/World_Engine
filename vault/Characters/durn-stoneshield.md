# Durn Stoneshield

*He does not like talking about the siege. He will talk about it for hours if you ask the right questions.*

---

## Background

Durn was born in the garrison quarters of Stonecrown and has spent nearly all of his life there. His father commanded the western wall section. His grandfather is credited with solving a drainage problem in the lower levels that had plagued the fortress for eighty years.

He took command at thirty-four and held the Siege of Stonecrown at thirty-eight. The siege lasted sixty days. He lost forty men. The attacking force lost several hundred. He considers this a satisfactory outcome with significant room for improvement.

---

## Story Role

Durn is the soldier's perspective on the Long Wars — not the politics, not the heroism, but the logistics, the responsibility, and the specific weight of being the person that a garrison of three hundred depends on.

---

## Notes

*His relationship with Eredan is complicated — Eredan arrived during the siege and offered three pieces of advice, none of which Durn took, all of which turned out to be correct. They have gotten on well ever since.*
